Galle

Introduction

Galle holds a significant position as a major city in Sri Lanka with its coastal beauty , vibrant cultural experiences and rich historical heritage. This UNESCO World Heritage Site Situated along the south coast of Sri Lanka. So, come let’s go on a journey around this magical city.

History

In late 16th century Galle was conquered by Portuguese. Then Galle was quickly overtaken by Dutch in 17th century and built their fortress now known as Galle Fort. After Dutch, in 1796 British took control of the Galle and make its their military center until Sri Lanka became an Independent nation in 1948.

Gall Fort

Galle Fort was built by Dutch in 17th century the fort strategically designed for protect the city from invasions. Fort has more Dutch architectural elements also Portuguese and British influence as well. In 1988 fort was recognized as UNESCO World Heritage Site. The museums and art galleries of Galle Fort shows rich history and importance of Galle.
